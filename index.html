THE REALM AWAITS
— ✦ — A Brief Counsel for the Newly Arrived — ✦ —
How the Realm Works
Crownfall is a shared roleplay set in 250 AC during the reign of Aegon V Targaryen. You play an original character — a lord, a knight, a merchant, a bastard — navigating politics, war, and intrigue in a world that does not care whether you survive.
The AI acts as your Game Master. It writes the world around you, controls every NPC, and resolves the consequences of your choices. It will not protect you. Characters can and do die.
The realm is shared. Other players exist in the same world. Their actions have consequences you may feel — and yours have consequences they may feel.
Making Choices
After each scene you will see 3–4 generated choices. At least one that looks safe is not. You can also write your own action — tap "write your own action" beneath the choices to type anything.
The AI resolves custom actions honestly, even if the result is fatal. Think before you act.
Gold & Stats
Your stats — Martial, Diplomacy, Intrigue, Stewardship, Learning — shape every outcome. The AI rolls dice for uncertain moments. High stats tip the odds; low stats do not make things impossible, just more dangerous.
Gold is real. Feasts, bribes, travel, and mercenaries all cost money. If you run out, you go into debt. Debts have consequences.
Moving Through the Realm
Travel happens through your actions — simply tell the GM you wish to travel somewhere and it will play out as a scene. You do not click a button or select a destination.
For example: "I make arrangements to depart for King's Landing at dawn" or "I ride hard for Winterfell, resting only when the horse demands it."
The GM will handle the journey as a scene — road conditions, weather, who you meet on the way. It will not simply skip you to the destination.
Travel Costs
Moving between locations costs gold automatically when your location changes. Rough costs:
• Within a city or castle — free
• Within a region — 5–15 gold dragons
• Across regions — 20–60 gold dragons
• Sea voyage — 30–80 gold dragons, with storm risk
Winter travel costs the same in gold but carries far greater danger in the narrative. A knight who cannot afford the road simply cannot leave.
Your Location on the Map
The map on the left panel shows your current position as a glowing gold dot. Small house sigils mark where other players currently are. If sigils cluster around your dot, you share a location with other characters.
Playing with Other Characters
When another player's character is at the same location as you, you can invite them into a shared scene. Both characters are then present in the same narrative — the GM sees you both and writes for both.
You cannot invite someone who is in a different location. They must travel to you — or you to them — first. This is intentional.
How to Start a Shared Scene
1. Look at the "Who Is Here" panel on the right side of the game screen. It lists every living character currently at your location.
2. Click the ⚔ invite button next to their name.
3. Confirm the invitation. They will receive a notification and can accept or decline.
4. Once they accept, a banner appears in both your scenes. Either of you can act. The GM writes for both characters.
Either player can leave a shared scene at any time using the "Leave Scene" button in the banner. The scene ends for both of you.
What the GM Sees
In a shared scene the GM knows both characters' names, houses, health, and traits. It will write NPCs who react to both of you. It will not speak for your partner — only for NPCs.
Sending Messages Across the Realm
Ravens allow you to send in-character messages to any other living character, regardless of where they are. A message you send as your character — not as a player — as though you had written a letter and sent it by bird.
To send a raven, click the Ravens button in the top bar of the game screen. Search for the recipient by character name, write your message, and dispatch it.
Ravens are the correct in-world mechanism for sharing information. If you want another character to know something — a warning, an alliance offer, a threat — a raven is how you tell them.
What Ravens Are Not
Ravens are not seen by the GM and do not affect your story directly. They are player-to-player communication. What your character does with the information in a raven — that plays out in your own scenes.
Write ravens in character. "Lord Stark — I write to warn you of movements in the Riverlands..." not "hey can you come to King's Landing".
Inscribing Your Deeds
After any scene, you will see a small link beneath the narrative — ✦ Inscribe this deed in the chronicles. Clicking it saves an excerpt of that scene to the public chronicle, readable by all players.
The chronicle is a living record of what has happened in the realm. Great deeds, deaths, betrayals, alliances — anything worth remembering can be inscribed.
You choose what to inscribe and what to keep private. Not every scene needs to be public. A conspiracy that goes into the chronicle is a conspiracy someone might read.
Reading the Chronicle
The Great Chronicle is accessible from the main page. It shows all inscribed deeds, filterable by house and location. It is the closest thing the realm has to shared history.
What you read there your character can know — formally inscribed deeds are public record. Rumours and private events that were never inscribed are not.
